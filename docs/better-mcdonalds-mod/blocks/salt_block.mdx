**Salt Block** is a gravity-affected [block](https://minecraft.wiki/w/Block) found in [rivers](https://minecraft.wiki/w/River), in patches in shallow surface water.

## Obtaining

### Breaking

Salt blocks can be mined with any item, but [shovels](https://minecraft.wiki/w/Shovel) are quickest. Breaking a salt block with a regular tool drops 2–4 [salt](../items/salt). The [Fortune](https://minecraft.wiki/w/Fortune) enchantment increases the chance of getting more [salt](../items/salt) within this range, but does not increase the maximum amount. Using a tool with [Silk Touch](https://minecraft.wiki/w/Silk_Touch) makes the block drop itself instead.

### Natural generation

Salt blocks are found at the bottoms of [rivers](https://minecraft.wiki/w/River) in [disk](https://minecraft.wiki/w/Disk)-like formations, disregarding adjacency to other types of blocks.

## Usage

If the supporting block below a salt block is removed, it falls until it lands on the next available block. More specifically, the salt block becomes a "[falling block](https://minecraft.wiki/w/Falling_block)" [entity](https://minecraft.wiki/w/Entity), which is affected by gravity. When the falling block lands on a [solid block](https://minecraft.wiki/w/Solid_block), it becomes a block again.
